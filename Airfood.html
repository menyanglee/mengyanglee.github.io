AirFood

Abstract

Geometric shape learning plays a critical role in children’s everyday reasoning and serves as a foundation for future STEAM education. In the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) community, it remains unclear how dynamic, shape-changing interfaces, such as morphing food, can facilitate young children’s understanding of geometric transformations. To investigate pneumatic morphing food as a medium for object-based learning of geometric concepts in kindergarten-aged children, we adopted the Research through Design (RtD) methodology to iteratively develop and refine the design. Specifically, we explored dough materials and pneumatic techniques to encourage children's hands-on engagement in learning geometric transformations. Based on our material exploration and expert insights, we developed the AirFood toolkit and conducted two rounds of user studies to test its usability and evaluate its educational potential. During the user studies, embodied interaction with morphing dough helped enhance children’s understanding of geometric shapes and transformations. Finally, we discuss findings and implications for designing transformation media in early childhood learning.

As the project is still under review, further details are not yet publicly available. If you are interested in learning more, feel free to reach out to me via email.
