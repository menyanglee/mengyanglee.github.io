Mengyang Lee


Hi! I'm Mengyang Lee, a Second-year graduate student in Industrial Design at Zhejiang University, currently working with Guanyun Lab where I collaborate with an amazing team to explore innovative design concepts. My research interests span Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), tangible interfaces, and wearable design. I am passionate about how emerging technologies can change the way we perceive and interact with the world, fostering more meaningful connections between humans and their surroundings.

My recent work focuses on how accessible conductive material can engage people in both wearable device creation and learning through interactive, low-cost experiences.

HydroSkin

This project explores a low-cost, time-efficient, and accessible fabrication technique for on-skin interfaces.By mixing carbon ink with sodium alginate, we developed a low-cost conductive material suitable for hydrographic printing technology. Compared to existing fabrication methods, our approach streamlines the fabrication process and utilizes cost-effective ink without requiring specialized skill.

Banshan Rural Revitalization

This project is a tailored service and strategy innovation design for Banshan Village in Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province. By leveraging the village's existing collective economy, homestays, and recycling systems, we developed a business strategy to promote the formation of a business environment alliance within the village. This strategy integrates economic prosperity with brand promotion, creating a comprehensive operational model. Additionally, we organized a series of zero-carbon activities to establish Banshan Village as a "Zero-Carbon Village," enhancing its reputation and sustainability.
