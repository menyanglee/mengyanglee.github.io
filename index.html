Mengyang Lee


Hi! I'm Mengyang Lee, a second-year graduate student in Design Science at Zhejiang University, currently working with Guanyun Lab where I collaborate with an amazing team to explore innovative design concepts. My research interests span Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), tangible interfaces, and sensory design. I am passionate about how emerging technologies can change the way we perceive and interact with the world, fostering more meaningful connections between humans and their surroundings.

My recent work focuses on how morphing food can engage children in both food creation and learning through interactive, fun experiences.

Feel free to reach out if you’d like to discuss research or brainstorm creative design ideas together! You can also check out my photography on Instagram.

MorphingScents

MorphingScents is a flexible, five-layer odor-emitting mechanism that uses Joule heating to release scents and drive shape changes, offering dynamic, aesthetic, and lightweight olfactory experiences through innovative scent release mechanisms and deformation techniques.

  • More

E-Orthosis

E-Orthosis is an innovative, cost-effective fabrication approach that enables healthcare professionals to quickly integrate electronics into off-the-shelf orthoses, offering customized functionalities to support rehabilitation, therapy, and health monitoring while reducing development time and cost.
